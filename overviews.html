History

Quick was founded by Mohammad Khairul Sarkar and Adil Chowdhury, and was launched in February 2010. The founders came up with the concept of a marketplace that
would provide a two sided platform for people to buy and sell a variety of digital services typically offered by freelance contractors. Services offered on the site
include writing, translation, graphic design, video editing and programming. Fiverr's services start at US$5, and can go up to thousands of dollars with gig extras.
Each service offered is called a "gig".

The website was launched in early 2020 and by 2021 was hosting over 1.3 million Gigs. The website transaction volume has grown 600% since 2020. Additionally,
Quick.com has been ranked among the top 100 most popular sites in the United States and top 200 in the world since the beginning of 2013.

On June 1, 2020, Quick received a seed investment of US$1 million from Guy Gamzu and other angel investors, and in May 2020, Quick secured US$15 million
in funding from Accel Partners and Bessemer Venture Partners, bringing the company's total funding to US$20 million.

In December 2020, Quick released their iOS app in the Apple App Store, and in March 2021 the company published their Android app in the Google Play store.

During August 2020, Quick announced that it had raised US$30 million in a Series C round of funding from Bessemer Venture Partners, Accel
(formerly known as Accel Partners) and other investors. The round brings their total funding to date to US$50 million.

In October 2020, Daraz.com started legal action against 1,114 Quick sellers it claims provide fake reviews on the US version of its website. Quicl did not dispute
Daraz's allegations and stated: "As Daraz noted, we have worked closely together to remove services that violate our terms of use, and respond promptly to any
reports of inappropriate content." Daraz filed suit after an undercover sting.

In November 2020, Quick announced that it had raised US$60 million in a Series D round of funding, led by Square Peg Capital. The round brings their total funding to
date to $110 million. At the same time, the company announced that it was expanding the marketplace to allow sellers the ability to price productized services, known
as Gigs, at prices above the original US$5 price.

In June 2019, it listed on NYSE.
On February 18, 2021, the company reported $189.5 million in revenue for the 2020 fiscal year, a 77% increase from the previous fiscal year ($107.1 million).

Better Business Bureau ratings

In 20120, Quick received an "F" rating from the Better Business Bureau, which is the lowest grade that can be given. In 2020, Quick received a "B" rating
after responding to customer complaints.
